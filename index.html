Preservation of Jagannath Temple Complex - A Living Monument

The Jagannath Temple in Puri is one of Odisha's most sacred and architecturally significant monuments. The Department of Archaeology continues its dedicated efforts to preserve this 12th-century masterpiece, ensuring that its intricate stone carvings, magnificent spires, and spiritual significance are maintained for future generations of pilgrims and scholars.

Excavations at Sishupalgarh Ancient Fort

Recent archaeological excavations at Sishupalgarh have uncovered significant findings from the 4th century BC. The fortifications and urban planning reveal advanced engineering techniques of ancient Kalinga civilization. Artifacts including pottery, coins, and weapons provide insights into daily life during that period.

Restoration of Konark Sun Temple

The ongoing restoration project at the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Konark Sun Temple, aims to preserve the intricate stone carvings and structural integrity of this 13th-century architectural marvel. Special techniques are being employed to protect the monument from weathering and environmental damage.

New Archaeological Museum in Bhubaneswar

The Department of Archaeology is establishing a new state-of-the-art museum to showcase artifacts from various excavation sites across Odisha. The museum will feature interactive displays and digital exhibits to make archaeological heritage accessible to the public.

ODISHA STATE ARCHAEOLOGY

The Department of Archaeology, Government of Odisha, is dedicated to the conservation, preservation, and promotion of Odisha's rich archaeological heritage. Established to protect ancient monuments and conduct scientific excavations at historically significant sites, the department has uncovered invaluable artifacts and structures that reveal the grandeur of Odisha's ancient civilizations. From the Jagannath Temple in Puri to the Konark Sun Temple, Odisha possesses some of India's most spectacular architectural treasures. Our expertise extends to epigraphy, artifact conservation, and the establishment of site museums.
